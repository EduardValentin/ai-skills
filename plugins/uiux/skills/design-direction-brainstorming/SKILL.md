# Design Direction Brainstorming

## Overview

Align on the design's job before choosing pixels. The goal is a short, decisive design brief that captures audience, intent, constraints, and the desired reaction from the user seeing the interface.

## When Direction Is Unclear

Pause before implementation when:

- Several aesthetics or layouts could reasonably fit.
- The user asks for broad improvement without a specific target.
- The change affects first impression, trust, perceived quality, or product positioning.
- The audience, workflow, or success criteria are unstated.
- The component may become a reusable pattern.

## Conversation Shape

Ask only what changes the design decision:

1. Who is the primary audience and what are they trying to do?
2. What should the surface make them feel or believe?
3. What is the business or product goal?
4. What constraints matter: density, speed, brand, accessibility, content length, device, or parity?
5. What should this avoid feeling like?

Then propose two or three distinct directions. Each direction should include:

- Core idea.
- Best fit.
- Tradeoff.
- Visual language: hierarchy, density, color rhythm, typography, motion, component shape.

## Decision Output

End with a compact brief:

```markdown
Chosen direction:
- Audience:
- Goal:
- Desired impression:
- Visual language:
- Interaction/motion:
- Accessibility and content constraints:
- Open questions:
```

Do not implement until the direction is clear enough that the next design choices are constrained.
